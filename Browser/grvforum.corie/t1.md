# For Newcomers: What Grav Is — and Why Everyone Hates You

**Author** LowOrbit&emsp;**Posted** 6 years ago&emsp;**Status** Pinned by moderation  

## Opening Post
If you’re reading this, you’re either new — or way too confident. In both cases, you’re in danger.  
  
Grav is not jumping.  
Grav is not “space parkour.”  
Grav is not a sport, a movement, or a lifestyle.  
  
Grav is stepping beyond what’s allowed — not for a goal, but for the act itself. You’re not breaking the law. You’re breaking the agreement that lets everyone else live more comfortably inside the cage.  
  
Why are we hated? Because we ruin the picture.  
  
The dome is supposed to be safe.  
The station — sealed.  
The orbit — controlled.  
  
And we are proof that all of it is temporary, conditional, and cracks under real pressure. Workers hate you because you mess with schedules. Corporates hate you because you introduce risk. USOL hates you because you exist outside their scenarios. But worst of all — gravers will hate you if you came here for adrenaline, likes, or the feeling of being “special.”  
  
If you want thrills — go to simulators.  
If you want fame — go stream.  
If you want freedom — remember this:  
  
Freedom here isn’t measured in distance. It’s measured by how many times you managed to come back. One last thing. If people are glad to see you here — you’re either dead, or you still haven’t understood where you are.
