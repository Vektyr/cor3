I’ve been seeing a recurring mistake in the shared run logs, esp at older industrial spots with partially active centrifuges. I think it’s an overcorrection of rd during the g pulses.  
  
  It’s instinctual to counter-spin aggressively and regain alignment when there’s a flux mid arc but that doesn’t work outside of stable micro. In an industrial field where gravity is tied to mech rotation that correction compounds into the deviation rather than stabilizing it.  
  
  In a lot of these older sites the g isn’t on or off its rotating with the structure and if you try to cancel your spin without accounting for the rotational source you’re fighting too much.  
  
  I would correct into the next phase instead of against the spin. Curious how others handle this, specifically at Broken Halo.
